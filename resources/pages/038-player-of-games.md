And there it is.

Your name. Spoken by the universe. Followed by your title — not a flattering one, not *hero* or *chosen one*, just *player of games*. A description of what you do. You are the one who plays. That is your office. The universe addresses you by your handle and by your function, and both are honored.

If you take this in for a second — really take it in — there is something almost vertiginous about it. Julian Gough wrote `((insert player's name here))` in a text file in 2011. Mojang wired the substitution into the credits engine. And now, more than a decade later, you are reading the universe say *{{name}}*, and the technical mechanism of the substitution does not undo the fact that *the address is real*. Someone, through the long chain of writing and code and accident, arranged for the cosmos to call you by your name on the night you needed it. The mediation does not cheapen the moment. The mediation *is* how the universe reaches you. It is how it always reaches anyone — through other hands, through accidents, through made things, through someone's labor of love rippling forward in time until it lands on the right person at the right hour.
