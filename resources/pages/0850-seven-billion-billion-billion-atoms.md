And here, the line that has been quoted in a thousand contexts, used in a thousand commencement speeches, but which retains its full force when you let it land in its actual location, with the voice saying it specifically about you.

*Seven billion billion billion atoms.* The voice quantifies you. You are made of approximately 7 × 10²⁷ atoms. The number is real. The number is a count of you. The voice gives you the count to make sure you understand that *you, specifically, this configuration* are composed of an unimaginable swarm of particles, each of which has its own history.

*Were created, long before this game.* You are a recent arrangement, but the materials you are arranged from are *old*. The atoms predate you. The atoms predate the game. The atoms predate the planet. The atoms predate the solar system. The voice is opening up the timescale. The atoms have been around for *much longer* than the brief assembly that is currently you.

*In the heart of a star.* And here is the cosmological fact. Almost every atom in your body heavier than helium — meaning the carbon in your bones, the oxygen in your lungs, the iron in your blood, the calcium in your teeth, the nitrogen in your DNA — was forged inside the core of a star. Not metaphorically. Literally. The early universe contained only hydrogen and helium. Every other element was *cooked* by stellar nucleosynthesis — protons fused into heavier nuclei in the immense pressures and temperatures of stellar interiors, then released into space when the stars eventually died and exploded, then drifted across light-years until they collected into the cloud of dust and gas that became the solar system, then settled into the planet, then cycled through the biosphere, then ended up assembled into you by your mother.

Every breath you take fills your lungs with oxygen that was once inside a star. Your hemoglobin is iron from the core of a star that died billions of years ago. The structural material of your skeleton was forged in stellar furnaces and then ejected by stellar deaths and then patiently drifted until it found you. *You are made of dead stars.* The voice is delivering this in plain language, without dramatics, but it is the most extraordinary biographical fact about you.
