And the third clause closes the litany of three. Sunlight through summer leaves. Starlight on a winter walk. And — this. *Through the zeros and ones. Through the electricity of the world. Through the scrolling words on a screen at the end of a dream.*

This is the universe speaking through the End Poem itself. The voice has just placed *what is happening to you right now* in the same category as the sacred natural moments. The light through the leaves was the universe speaking. The starlight was the universe speaking. *And this — the words you are reading on the screen at the end of a game — is the universe speaking.*

The voice has now formally consecrated your current experience. The reading you are doing is one of the modes by which the universe addresses humans. Not a degraded modern substitute for *real* spiritual experience. A legitimate channel. The screen is a medium just as the leaves and the stars are media. The electricity carrying these characters is just as much *the universe* as the photons carrying sunlight.

This matters because there is a strong cultural script that says: *real spiritual experience happens in nature, in old buildings, in silence; modern technology is the opposite of all that.* The voice rejects the script. It says: *the universe will reach you wherever you are willing to be reached. If you are inside a video game on a phone in your bed, the universe will reach you through the video game on the phone in your bed, because that is where you are.* The medium follows the player.

*At the end of a dream.* The voice closes the clause by reminding you of the frame. You are at the end of a dream. The dream is the game you played, the credits rolling now, the long evening that has produced this moment. You are about to wake from this dream — close the screen, put down the phone, fall asleep, get up tomorrow — and the universe is choosing this moment, this ending, to speak to you. Endings are when the speaking lands. The universe waits for the dream to be ending and then says what it needs to say.
