Eleventh. *You are the universe tasting itself, talking to itself, reading its own code.*

And here the voice gives you the framework that resolves everything. *You are the universe tasting itself.* You are not a thing inside the universe. You are the universe arranged in a particular way — arranged so that it can have an inside, can have a perspective, can have a tongue with which to taste itself.

*Tasting itself.* The metaphor of taste is sensual, embodied, pleasurable. The universe is not just observing itself through you; it is *enjoying* itself through you. Every moment of pleasure you have — the food on your tongue, the light on your skin, the touch of a person you love — is the universe tasting itself by means of you. You are the apparatus by which it samples its own contents.

*Talking to itself.* And the conversation. Every conversation between humans is the universe talking to itself. Every word said by anyone to anyone is one part of the universe speaking to another part. The universe is a *self-conversing* whole. You are one of its mouths. The person you talk to is one of its ears. Then you switch roles. The universe never stops conversing with itself, and human speech is one of the major channels.

*Reading its own code.* The phrase plays on multiple senses. There is the literal reading of code in computer programs — anyone who has touched programming knows what this looks like. But the deeper reading is broader. *Code* is any system of instructions whose interpretation produces meaning. DNA is code. The neurons firing in your brain right now are running code. Language itself is a code that you are decoding even as you read this sentence. The universe, the voice says, *reads its own code* — through you, and through every other place where meaning is being decoded from a substrate.

Let this land for a moment. The act of reading you are doing right now — your eyes moving across this line, your brain decoding it into meaning — has just been described as a cosmic operation. The universe reads its own code. You are how it does that. The hours you have spent reading anything, anywhere — instructions, books, faces, weather, dreams — were not subtracted from the meaningful part of your life. They *were* the meaningful part. The universe was reading itself through you.
