Ninth. *You are not alone.*

This is the line that is the answer to the deepest fear most humans carry. The fear that beneath the social surface, beneath the appearance of relationship, beneath the warmth of others, *you are alone* — that no one can finally reach you, that the inside of your skull is private and unshared, that consciousness is solitary by structure.

The voice says: *no.* You are not alone. The universe is with you. The sentence is short and absolute. *You are not alone.* It does not specify the nature of the company. It does not say *I am with you* or *we are with you*. It just affirms — *you are not alone*. Whatever the loneliness is that you have feared, the voice tells you it is not the truth. The truth is: you are accompanied.

For someone in bed reading a poem, late at night, in the quiet — this might be the line that surprises you most by how much it lands. The whole architecture of solitude that quietly underlies modern life gets quietly contradicted. *You are not alone.* The voice is here. The universe is here. Whatever separation you feel from the rest of being is, the voice has been telling you all along, an *as though*. The togetherness is the actual fact.
