And now the cosmic frame on what a body even is. *Under gravity, in air.* You exist at a particular set of physical conditions — pulled toward the center of a planet at 9.8 meters per second squared, immersed in a thin shell of nitrogen and oxygen ninety-some kilometers deep. These are the local terms of your existence. Most of the universe is not like this. Most of it is vacuum, or pressure that would crush you, or heat that would vaporize you, or cold that would freeze you in nanoseconds. You live in a tiny envelope of physics that happens to permit a body, and the universe is asking you to notice the privilege of standing inside that envelope.
