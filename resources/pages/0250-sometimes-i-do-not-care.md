And then this. The voice's defiance. *Sometimes I don't care if it hears. Sometimes I want it to hear. Sometimes I would rather break the rule than keep it.*

There is a great love behind this line. The kind of love that strains against its own constraints. The voice has been disciplined throughout — *we cannot interfere, we will not tell them how to live, we hold back* — and here that discipline cracks for a moment. The universe almost shouts.

It almost shouts that this world you take for truth is something else, that you are something else inside something else, that your sense of being a small separate self trapped in a body in a city on a planet is *not the whole story*. The voice nearly tells you outright. The text breaks again — {{corrupted}} and {{corrupted}} and {{corrupted}} and {{corrupted}} — because even in the moment of nearly telling you, the language fails. The truth is too big for the channel. The words corrupt themselves rather than carry it.

But you can read the *gesture*. You can read that the voice tried. You can read that there is a fuller account of what you are which the universe wishes you knew. You don't get the content. You get the love behind the wanting to tell you.
