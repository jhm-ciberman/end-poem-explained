And the voice closes the loop.

Earlier in the poem the voice described sunlight as *information from a star*. Now it tells you that *you* are also information from a star. The same vocabulary. The same kind of thing. Sunlight is a transmission from a star. You are also a transmission from a star — slower, more circuitous, more elaborate, but a transmission nonetheless.

Stars, in this account, are *broadcasting*. They radiate light, which is information; they synthesize heavy elements, which are also information, and which they release into the galaxy when they die. Some of that information collects into planets. Some of that information assembles into living things. Some of that information becomes you, in your bed at this particular hour, reading words on a screen.

You are a long, slow message from a star you will never see. The star is dead. The message is alive. You are what the star said.
