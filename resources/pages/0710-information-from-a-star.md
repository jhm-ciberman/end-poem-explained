And here the voice closes the long-distance shot with a small sting. *It could burn your skin.*

The vast benevolent sun, the warmth-giver, the parent, is also — at the energy levels involved — a thing that could destroy you. The same light that grew the food you ate is, in slightly more concentrated form, lethal. You have probably had a sunburn. That is the universe touching you a little too hard. The thing eight minutes away is so violent that even its diluted reach can hurt you.

The phrase *information from a star* is doing important quiet work. The voice frames sunlight not just as energy but as *information*. Light is signal. Light is data. The star is *transmitting*, and your skin is *receiving*. Photosynthesis is decoding. Vision is decoding. Your tan is a written record of solar transmission. You are, biologically, in active correspondence with a star.

And note what just happened to your idea of *yourself*. Three sentences ago you *thought yourself human*. Now you have been recharacterized as a small thin-skinned creature on a precarious crust around a vast incandescent radiation source whose information has been writing itself onto your body since the moment you were born. *You* are still in there somewhere. But the *you* the universe is describing is much stranger and more interesting than the you who put on a t-shirt this morning.
