The decision. *Not the truth. A story.* The voice has chosen its method. It cannot give you the naked truth — that's settled. But it can wrap the truth in narrative, in image, in metaphor, and hand you the package. You will receive the truth without recognizing it as truth. You will receive it as story, which means you can hold it without being burned by it.

This is what every wisdom tradition has done. Jesus speaks in parables not because he can't speak directly but because the parable preserves the listener's freedom to receive or refuse. The Buddha tells stories of past lives. The Sufis embed metaphysics in love poems and drinking songs. The Hasidic masters tell tales whose meaning the listener must work out themselves. Plato writes dialogues, not treatises. The story is not a lesser version of the truth. The story is the *only* version of the truth that the human mind can receive without distortion.
