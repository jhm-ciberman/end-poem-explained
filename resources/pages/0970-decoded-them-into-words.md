This is one long unfolding of a single act. *Decoded them into words.* The lines of code — the raw symbols on the screen — get parsed into recognizable words. The eye sees marks; the brain assembles them into language. That is the first decoding.

*Decoded words into meaning.* The words, once recognized, get translated into semantic content. *The cat sat on the mat* is not just five words; it is a scene, a small picture of an animal on a fabric, a state of affairs. The brain performs the second decoding — from words into meaning.

*Decoded meaning into feelings, emotions, theories, ideas.* And then the third decoding. The meaning, once received, produces interior weather. You read a sentence and feel sad. You read a sentence and form a theory. You read a sentence and have an idea. The meaning is converted into the actual stuff of inner life — affect, cognition, motivation.

The voice is breaking down a process that normally happens in milliseconds and is invisible to introspection. You read a sentence and you do not notice that *three separate decoding operations* just occurred. The voice slows it down so you can see the machinery. You are a *decoder*. The world hands you symbols and you turn them into experience.

This is also a quiet mirror of *what the universe has been doing throughout the poem*. The universe encoded the truth into a story; the story into words; the words into pixels on your screen. The voice is asking you to notice that the same operation runs in reverse: pixels into words; words into meaning; meaning into feeling. The encoding and the decoding are the same operation seen from opposite ends. The universe and you are *in conversation*, and the conversation works because you can decode what the universe encodes.
