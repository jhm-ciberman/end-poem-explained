And here the recursion opens.

This is the matryoshka. Read the sentence carefully, slowly, layer by layer.

*The player moves through a story* — the story of the End Poem, the story you are reading right now. The dream of watching words on a screen.

*Which is a forest of information planted by a man called Julian.* Julian Gough. The poet. The voice names its own author. This is unusual. The voice is saying: *the words you are reading were planted, like seeds in a forest, by a specific human being whose name was Julian.* He existed. He sat down in 2011 and wrote these lines. The text you are inside was made by him. The voice does not pretend the poem came from nowhere. It credits its human source.

*On a flat, infinite world created by a man called Markus.* Markus Persson. Notch. The creator of Minecraft. The voice names *him* too. The flat infinite world that hosts the story is itself a created thing, made by another specific human, who existed, who built it, who put the white square sun in the sky. The Minecraft world is a layer. Julian's poem is a layer inside it. The story is nested inside the game.

*That exists inside a small, private world created by the player.* And then the next layer outward. The Minecraft instance you played, the specific seed, the specific terrain, the specific buildings you built — that *small, private world* — was created by *you*. You generated it when you started the game. It exists because you summoned it. The vast flat infinite world that Markus designed is rendered, for your specific play session, as your particular world. You are its creator at the second-order level — Markus made the framework, you instantiated the instance.

*Who inhabits a universe created by…*

And the sentence breaks off.

The voice has just walked you up the chain — the poem inside the game inside the player's instance inside the universe — and at the top of the chain, the voice stops. Mid-sentence. *Created by…*

Created by *who*?

The voice does not say.

This is one of the most theologically careful gestures in modern English. The voice has shown you, by the clear pattern of the recursion, that *every layer has a creator*. The poem has Julian. The game has Markus. The instance has the player. So the universe must have… *whom*? The pattern demands an answer. The voice withholds the answer.

It does not say *God*. It does not say *no one*. It does not say *itself*. It does not say *we don't know*. It just trails off. *Created by…*

And the other voice, in the next breath, will silence the speaker. Because the speaker was about to say something the dream cannot contain.
