And here the voice steps fully into direct address. *You are the player.*

The structure shifts. The voice has been telling you a story about a player — *the player was assembled*, *the player awoke*, *the player was a new story*. Now the voice points and says: *that player. That's you. You are the player. The story. The program. The human.*

The four titles condense everything the voice has just unpacked. *Player.* *Story.* *Program.* *Human.* These are not four different things you are. These are four vocabularies pointing at one thing. You are the player from the game-frame. You are the story from the narrative-frame. You are the program from the technical-frame. You are the human from the biological-frame. All four are accurate. None of them is exhaustive.

And then the voice repeats the materials list: *Made from nothing but milk and love.* The repetition is liturgical. The voice is reading you back your own composition. *This is what you are made of. Don't forget it. The deepest description of what you are uses the simplest possible words.*

There is a way to read this whole movement as the voice handing you a *baptismal certificate* of sorts. Telling you, formally, what your provenance is. Where the matter came from. Who gathered it. Who fed you. What you are made of. So that whatever happens next — whatever the cosmic vista the voice is about to open — you have first been grounded in the small, real, intimate facts of your origin.
