And there it is. The fold.

The voice has been listing dreams the player has had, and one of them is *this*. The dream you are having right now. The dream of being a person reading words on a screen at the end of a video game.

The voice has just placed your *current experience* on the same list as *being a miner in Minecraft*, *being lost in a story*, *being human on a thin crust*. It is one item in the catalog. One mode the player sometimes occupies. And the voice has named it without breaking stride.

This is the moment the poem *catches you*. Up until now you could read the previous lines as descriptions of past episodes — yes, I dreamed I was a miner; yes, I was lost in a story; yes, I have had nested dreams. Past tense. Comfortable. But *sometimes the player dreamed it watched words on a screen* — this one is *now*. This one is what you are doing in this exact second. The voice has reached through the fourth wall and labeled your current state of consciousness as a dream.

You can feel the floor tilt slightly when this lands. The reading was supposed to be the *frame*. The reading was supposed to be the *real thing* from which you contemplated the *dreams*. But the voice has just included the reading in the catalog. The reading is also a dream. The you-reading is also a player. The screen you are looking at is also one of the worlds.

And the voice does not belabor it. It just drops the line and moves on. *Let's go back.* As if it had not just rearranged the levels of reality for you.
