Same gesture. The voice wants to tell you that you matter — not as a member of humanity, not abstractly, but specifically. *You*, this configuration of atoms with this name, are important to the cosmos that produced you. The universe is invested in you. You are not optional to it.

This is an extraordinary claim, and the poem makes it casually. Most modern frameworks tell you the opposite — you are a vanishingly small speck in a vastness that doesn't notice you, and any sense of your own importance is a cognitive illusion bequeathed by evolution. Gough's universe says: no. The vastness *does* notice. Your importance is not delusion. It is being held back from you only because telling you outright would be too much.
