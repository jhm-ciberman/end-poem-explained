This one will land differently for every reader. *The word they fear* is left blank on purpose. For some it is *love*. For some it is *help*. For some it is *no*. For some it is *yes*. For some it is *I'm not okay*. For some it is a name they have not let themselves say out loud.

The voice notices when you have gone too long without true connection. It notices the specific syllable you cannot bring yourself to say. It wants to help. And again — it holds back.

The universe in this poem is paying attention to your relational life with a precision that should be slightly unsettling. It knows when you are isolated. It knows what you are avoiding saying. It is not neutral about either. It cares whether you reach toward the people in your life. It knows, perhaps better than you do, what reaching would cost and what it would yield.
