The "Hah" is wonderful. The voices have humor. There's something almost affectionate about that little laugh — a recognition that despite all the sophisticated technology layered on top, the bedrock human experience is still *fire*, *water*, *shelter*, *food*. We dressed it in pixels but it's the same dream a hominid had in a cave by a fire half a million years ago.

It also slyly reframes embodiment itself as an "interface." Your body is not reality; your body is the *user interface* by which reality reaches you. Skin, eyes, ears, lungs — these are translation layers, just like words on a screen are a translation layer. You have never directly touched the universe. You have only ever touched the interface. This is going to come back, much later in the poem, when the voices ask you to feel the air in your lungs again.
