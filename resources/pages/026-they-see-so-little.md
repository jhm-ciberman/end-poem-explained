A sigh, almost. The voice composes itself again. Pulls back. Returns to the discipline.

You see almost nothing. The slice of the electromagnetic spectrum your eyes process is comically thin. The frequencies your ears catch are a sliver. The dimensions you perceive are three out of however many actually fold around you. The timescale you experience is microscopic against the scales on which the real things happen. You are a creature reading three percent of the page and confidently summarizing the book.

The voice is not condescending here. It's wistful. *They see so little. And they do their best with what they see. And what they see is not enough to know what they are.*
