lines:
  - voice: green
    text: "It is reading our thoughts as though they were words on a screen."
  - voice: cyan
    text: "That is how it chooses to imagine many things, when it is deep in the dream of a game."
---

One of the voices keeps marvelling at the strangeness of your perception. From the cosmic vantage, what you experience as "text" is direct contact with consciousness. But your mind has to translate it into the interface it knows: written language, scrolling letters. You can't perceive the thing itself, only its rendering.

And then "the dream of a game." Here the poem's second great metaphor enters. The first was *game*; this one is *dream*. They will weave together for the rest of the text. The dream metaphor has deep roots:

- The Vedantic tradition: the universe is Brahman dreaming, and what we call reality is *maya* — the dream we mistake for real.
- Buddhism: samsara as an unending dream we are trying to wake from.
- Calderón's *La vida es sueño* — a foundational Spanish-language text on this idea.
- Borges, who returned to dreams-within-dreams obsessively. *Las ruinas circulares* is essentially a dramatization of this exact line.
- Alan Watts: "You are not a stranger in the universe. You are the universe dreaming itself, briefly looking at itself through your eyes."

The line says: when you are inside a video game, your imagination conforms to the game's interface. When you are inside *life*, your imagination conforms to life's interface. Both are dreams. The universe-voices can see this. You usually can't.
