And now, abruptly, the voices stop discussing and start *acting*. *Give it a body.* The decision has been made. The story will be told. But for the story to be told, you need to be returned to yourself.

You have been disembodied throughout the credits sequence. You died, the dragon died, the credits rolled, you became something more like a witness than a person. The universe is now reincorporating you. Putting you back inside the suit of skin. Returning you to the interface.

This is a tiny version of the central act of every incarnational mythology. Spirit takes flesh so that something can happen here. You are about to be sent back into the dream so that the story can do its work in the place where you actually live, which is not in a transcendent overhearing but in a body that breathes.
