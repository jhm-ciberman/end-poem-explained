    text: "The words change. We do not change."

And this is the rest stop. The voice summarizes what it has just said. The history of human contact with the larger reality has been a history of *re-naming*. The names have evolved with culture, with literacy, with technology, with politics. But the cosmic reality being named has not changed.

This is a profoundly stabilizing claim. It says: you do not need to be anxious about which religious tradition has the right name. They are all reaching for the same thing. And you do not need to be anxious that science has dissolved the sacred. Science has only renamed it. The thing under the names is what it always was.

It also gently undoes a lot of the loneliness of the modern condition. A common modern feeling is that the old religious vocabulary has lost its purchase but no new vocabulary has arrived to replace it, and so we live in a disenchanted world. The poem says: no. The world is not disenchanted. The vocabulary just rotated. The thing the old vocabulary was reaching for is still here, still in contact with you, still available — *under different names*, including the scientific ones, including the ones that have not been invented yet.
