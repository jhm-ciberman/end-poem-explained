    text: "I like this player. It played well. It did not give up."

A small benediction. Notice what the universe approves of. Not that you were clever, virtuous, beautiful, or successful. That you *played well* and *did not give up*. That's the entire criterion. Persistence in the face of difficulty.

This may land in a particular way depending on where you are. If you have ever kept showing up to something difficult — a habit you were trying to build, a piece of work that took longer than you expected, a relationship you stayed in through a hard season, a recovery that took years, a small daily act that no one else was watching — that is what the voice is honoring. Gough is building a theology of showing up. The universe in this poem doesn't ask whether you won. It asks whether you kept going.
