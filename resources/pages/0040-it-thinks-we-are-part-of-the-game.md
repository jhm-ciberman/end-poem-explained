This is one of the quietly devastating lines. The second voice waves off the warning. Why isn't it dangerous that you're reading their private conversation? Because *you'll dismiss it as fiction*. You'll close the laptop and say: that was a beautiful little ending, what a nice writer Mojang hired. The frame of "this is just a game" protects the truth from being too dangerous to receive.

This is a profoundly old idea. Sacred truths get smuggled inside stories because the naked version would burn or break the receiver. Jesus speaks in parables. The Sufis hide divine love inside love poems about wine and women. Plato has his noble lie. Kierkegaard called it "indirect communication." The poem is announcing its own method here: *I am about to tell you something true. I am going to tell it to you disguised as a video game ending. That way you can hear it.*
