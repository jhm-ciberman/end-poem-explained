Now the voice shifts again — into longing. Into what it *wants* to do but holds back from doing.

It wants to tell you that the work matters. That what you build inside the dream is real on the outside of the dream. That the apparently small things — the conversations, the kindnesses you didn't have to give, the practice you do alone, the patient day-by-day showing-up — are constructing something true in a place you cannot see.

This is the universe wanting to give you the one piece of information that would change everything if you fully believed it: *your life is not rehearsal*. The thing being built is real. The hours are not wasted. Every act of care, every kept promise, every refused shortcut is laying down structure in a reality that outlasts you.

But the voice doesn't tell you. It only admits it wants to.
