This is one of the most precise descriptions of depression I have ever read.

Look at what it identifies. First: the depressed mind *creates* the world it then suffers in. The sadness is not received from outside; it is generated from within and projected outward, until the inner weather becomes the entire sky. *It creates worlds that have no summer.* You build a season-less place inside your head and then live in it.

Second: *it shivers under a black sun*. The black sun is an old alchemical and surrealist symbol — the *sol niger*, the dark light. It's the sun that gives no warmth. It's the presence of the source without any of the source's gift. A depressed person is not in the dark; that would be simpler. They are under a sun that does not warm them. The light is technically there. It just doesn't reach.

Third — and this is the cruelest part — *it takes its sad creation for reality*. This is the diagnostic core. The pain of depression isn't just feeling bad. It's *believing the bad feeling is the truth*. It's thinking: this isn't a mood; this is what life actually is, and the times I felt otherwise were the illusion. The depressed mind doesn't know it's wearing tinted glasses. It thinks it has finally seen things clearly.

The universe sees this. It sees you do this. It does not judge you for doing it. It just names it, with extraordinary accuracy, so that perhaps next time you do it you might recognize yourself in the description and remember: *oh — this is the part where I take my sad creation for reality.*
