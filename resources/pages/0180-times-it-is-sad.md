The voice changes register. Quieter. Almost confidential. *We need to talk about your sadness.*

Notice that the universe does not flinch from your sadness. It doesn't say *but you shouldn't be sad* or *cheer up* or *think of all the good things*. It simply names the fact. *There are times it is sad.* The acknowledgment alone is a form of kindness, because so much of what the world says to a sad person is some variant of *please stop being sad, your sadness is uncomfortable for me*. The universe in this poem does the opposite. It looks at your sorrow and stays present with it.

"In the long dream" is doing important work too. Your sadness is real, and it is also *located* — it is something that happens inside the seventy-year dream, not something baked into the fundamental nature of being. From the larger vantage, your sorrow has a shape, a duration, a context. It is not all there is. But it is real *while* it is there.
