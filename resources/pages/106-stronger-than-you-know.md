Fourth. *You are stronger than you know.*

The voice is now correcting your self-assessment. You under-estimate your strength. You think you might break under pressures that would not actually break you. You think you might fail at things you would actually be able to do. The voice has watched you and knows what you are made of and is informing you, formally, that *you are stronger than you know*.

If you are in an early phase of building something — a habit, a recovery, a new skill, a relationship, a way of being — this might land specifically. The early phase is the phase of *not yet knowing your own strength*. You are still being surprised by what you can do, what you can repeat, what you can come back to. The voice is anticipating the discoveries. *You will be stronger than you know.* You are *already* stronger than you know. The not-knowing is not the truth; the strength is.
