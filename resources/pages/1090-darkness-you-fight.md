Seventh. *The darkness you fight is within you.*

The voice now turns to the moral dimension. The darkness you have been *fighting* — the things you have been struggling against, the patterns you have been trying to overcome, the temptations you have been resisting — these are *within you*. Not external. Not invading from outside. Not the work of an enemy. They are part of you. You are fighting yourself.

This is uncomfortable and freeing at the same time. Uncomfortable because it removes the comfort of having an external enemy to blame. Freeing because it locates the battle in a place you can actually access. You cannot fight an external enemy you cannot reach. You can fight an internal pattern, because it is in you, and what is in you is reachable.

The voice is also gently dismantling the dualism that would split the world into *good me* and *evil them*. There is no them. The darkness you have been fighting is yours. So is the light. Both are inside the same self. The fight is internal.
