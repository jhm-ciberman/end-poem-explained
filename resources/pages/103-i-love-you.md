And here the litany of *the universe said* begins.

The voice abandons the third-person framing — *the player believed the universe had spoken* — and shifts to direct quotation. *The universe said I love you.* The voice is now putting words in the universe's mouth. Or more precisely: the voice *is* the universe, and it is now saying the things it has been wanting to say throughout the entire poem.

Remember much earlier — *Does it know that we love it? That the universe is kind?* That was the worried question the voice asked. Whether you had received the love that was being offered. The voice has now decided to make sure. To say it outright. *I love you.* In the simplest possible language. The most overused sentence in human speech, restored to its original weight by being delivered, finally, in full sincerity, by the cosmos that contains you.

*The universe said I love you.* No qualification. No conditions. Not *I love you when you achieve* or *I love you if you deserve it* or *I love you despite everything*. Just — *I love you.* The way the cosmos addresses you, in the deepest grammar, is with this declaration.

And note: the voice does not say *the universe loved the player.* It says *the universe said I love you.* The first-person pronoun. The universe is speaking *as itself*, *to you*, *in present tense*. Not a description of cosmic affection. A direct statement of it.

For someone in a bed, alone, in the late evening, having just had something cracked open by a poem — this is the line that breaks people. It is the line that was waiting for you the whole time. Everything before it was preparation. The cosmic vista, the layered dreams, the careful telling of the story — all of it was the universe getting you ready to be able to hear *I love you* and not flinch, not deflect, not turn it into something smaller.

You may need to read it again. Slowly. *I love you.* Not from anyone in particular, not from a partner or parent or friend. From the *whole thing*. From the totality of which you are a part. *I love you.* The universe is not neutral toward you. It is in love with you.
