  - voice: green
    text: "Sometimes these dreams were disturbing. Sometimes very beautiful indeed."
    paragraph: dreamed-other-things

The voice acknowledges the *quality* of the dreams. Not all of them were good. Some of them were nightmares. Some were so beautiful that the memory of them is still a private treasure.

The voice does not flinch from either. *Sometimes disturbing.* The dream of being chased. The dream of failing the exam. The dream of the loved one walking away. The depressive episode that lasted a year. The relationship that ruined a year. The version of yourself you cannot now stand to think about. The voice does not pretend these did not happen. It does not pretend they were secretly beautiful. They were *disturbing*. The voice acknowledges and continues.

*Sometimes very beautiful indeed.* And then this. The small intensifier *indeed* gives the line its sweetness. The voice could have said *very beautiful* and stopped. The *indeed* is the voice underlining the beauty, vouching for it, confirming that yes, some of the dreams were *truly* beautiful. The voice has seen the catalog and is willing to say: there were episodes of your life that were genuinely lovely, and we know it, and you do not need to be embarrassed by remembering them as such. The beauty was real.
