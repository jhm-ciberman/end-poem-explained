The voice asks itself the question, on your behalf. You have been listening to two voices for the entire poem. You have been spoken about by them, addressed by them, breathed back into your body by them. And until this moment you have not been told who *they* are.

The asking of the question is itself a gift. The voice could have left you wondering. Instead it turns to face the natural question and answers it directly. *Who are we?* — let me tell you.

There is also something philosophically beautiful about *we* asking *who are we*. A unified self does not normally ask itself who it is; it just is. The question implies a kind of self-reflection, a folding back of consciousness on itself. The universe is performing the same act of self-knowing that you perform every time you ask *who am I*. The cosmos is wondering at itself. And in wondering at itself, it becomes a thing that can be wondered at.
