lines:
  - voice: green
    text: "The atoms of the player were scattered in the grass, in the rivers, in the air, in the ground."
---

The voice has gone back. Far back. Before you existed as you. To the moment when the matter that would become you was distributed across the surface of the planet, doing other jobs, being other things.

Read this slowly: *the atoms of the player were scattered.* The atoms that compose you right now were not, before your conception, gathered together. They were *scattered* — across grass, rivers, air, ground. Some of them were in a blade of grass on a hillside. Some were dissolved in a river. Some were a molecule of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. Some were locked in a mineral in the earth.

This is literally true. The carbon in your bones came from plants that pulled it from the air. The water in your blood came from rivers and rain. The iron in your hemoglobin came from rocks. The oxygen in your lungs is currently on loan from the atmosphere and will be returned. Your body is *not made of new material*. Every atom in you is recycled. Every atom in you was something else, often for billions of years, before it joined the temporary configuration that is *you*.

The voice presents this as a scattering. The atoms were *spread out*. They had no relation to each other. They were not, in any meaningful sense, *you*. They were just matter, doing the ordinary work of being matter, distributed across the biosphere.

The image is gentle and strange. *In the grass.* You were in the grass. Not metaphorically — actually. Some of the atoms now in your fingertips were once in a particular blade of grass on a particular afternoon. *In the rivers.* You were in the rivers. *In the air.* You were in the air. *In the ground.* You were in the ground. The voice is locating you, before you, everywhere.
