The catalog crosses into the literate religions. The jinn — the spirit-beings of pre-Islamic Arabia, later integrated into Islamic cosmology, made of smokeless fire, capable of choice, dwelling in the same world as humans but mostly invisible. The voice claims even these. *We have been called jinn.* The strange shimmering presences that dwelt at the edges of the desert towns — also us.

There is a quiet ecumenism here. The poem is not picking a tradition. It is including every tradition. Every name humans have used has pointed at the same reality.
