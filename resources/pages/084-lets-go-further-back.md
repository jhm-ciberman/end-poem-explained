And then this. The voice is not done.

You might have thought *that was the going-back*. You started with the atoms scattered in grass and rivers, walked forward to your mother, your assembly, your birth, your composition. That seems like the full origin story. That seems like *enough*.

But the voice says *let's go further back.* The chain has not yet been followed all the way. The atoms in the grass and the rivers — where did *those* come from? Before they were in the grass, where were they? The voice is going to take you behind the biosphere, to the prior step, the deeper origin. *Further back.*

There is an ethical quality to this. The voice will not let you settle for a partial accounting. You have not yet seen the bottom. Most people, asked where they came from, will answer with their parents and stop there. Some will go to their grandparents. Some, in a more philosophical mood, will go to the evolution of humans from earlier primates, or to the abiogenesis of the first cells. The voice is going to take you further than any of those. It is going to take you to *stars*.
