A second instruction. *Feel.* Not think about, not imagine — *feel*. The poem is pulling you out of the cognitive register where you have been receiving it and into the sensory register where you actually live. You have been a reader; you are being asked to become, again, a body.

The air in your lungs is a remarkable thing to attend to. You breathe in something invisible, of which you understand only the chemistry, which has just come from the trees outside your window, which contains atoms that have circulated through countless other lungs across deep time, including the lungs of everyone you have ever loved and everyone who has ever lived. Your lungs are, at this very second, doing the same thing every set of human lungs has done for as long as there have been humans. *Feel air in your lungs* is an instruction to notice the ongoing miracle that is happening for free, without your effort, while you read about it.
