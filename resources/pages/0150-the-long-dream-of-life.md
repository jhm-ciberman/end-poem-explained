And here are the poem's two dreams, named explicitly for the first time. There is the *short dream of a game* — the few hundred hours you spent in Minecraft, or in any game, or in any temporary engagement. And there is the *long dream of life* — the seventy or eighty or ninety years you are walking through right now.

The short dream cannot give you the highest level. No game can. No engineered experience can. You can grind achievements forever and never level up *here*, in the place that counts. The leveling-up of the long dream happens through the slow texture of actual living: the sustained relationships, the work that takes years, the habit you are slowly forming, the patience with another person, the long deliberation over a hard choice, the showing up. Spiritual development cannot be speedrun.

This is the bodhisattva's understanding of awakening. It is the desert fathers' understanding of contemplation. It is what every wisdom tradition eventually arrives at: there is no shortcut, and there is no graduation without time.
