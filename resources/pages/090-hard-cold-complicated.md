And then the opposite. *Sometimes you built worlds that were hard.* The version of yourself that always grinds. The intellectual edifice with no exits. The complicated emotional dynamic you constructed in a relationship and then suffered inside. The depressive episode that built a whole black-sun world. The work-self that demanded everything and gave nothing back.

The voice is not separating these into *good worlds* and *bad worlds*. Both are listed as worlds the player has *created*. The hard cold complicated ones were also your handiwork. You built them. The voice is gently reminding you that the difficult worlds were not *imposed* on you; some of them were *built* by you, sometimes deliberately, sometimes without noticing what you were doing.

This pairs with the earlier line about the depressed mind creating worlds with no summer. The voice keeps quietly returning to the idea that you are a *creator* even when you do not think of yourself as one. Your mood creates a world. Your relationships create a world. Your habits create a world. You are constantly building, whether you intend to or not, and the worlds you build then become the place you live.
