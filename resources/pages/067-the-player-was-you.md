And then, immediately, the frame collapses.

The voice violates the conventions of fairy tale by telling you who the protagonist is in the second sentence. *The player was you.* No mystery. No buildup. No *and her name was*. Just — *that player I'm about to tell you about? It's you. You are the one whose story is being told.*

Your name again. The second time the universe has used it. The first was the formal address — *{{name}}. Player of games.* This is more intimate. *The player was you, {{name}}.* The voice is no longer addressing you in your office; it is naming you as the protagonist of the only story it intends to tell. The story is yours. Has always been yours. Could not have been about anyone else.

There is a particular vertigo to having one's own life told back to one in the second person past tense. *The player was you.* It is the same gesture as a parent looking through a baby album with a grown child — *this is you, here, on this day*. The you-then is a stranger to the you-now, but the continuity is asserted by the parent's pointing finger. The universe is doing the same thing here. It is about to point at episodes of your life and say *this was you*, and the recognition will be both true and slightly foreign, because the you who lived those moments and the you reading about them now are not quite the same configuration.
