A small line, almost a stage direction. The voice notices that you've hit the limit. The translation layer can't carry this one. *Don't worry about explaining it; they can't receive it anyway.*

There's something tender about this. The voices are not being cagey. They tried to tell you. The medium failed. The kindness is in trying anyway, knowing that even the corrupted message — the *shape* of an unspeakable thing — is itself a form of contact. You know now that *something* is there, even if you cannot read what.
