This is the first hinge of the poem, and the reason the End Poem hits so much harder than people expect. Julian Gough wrote the original with `((insert player's name here))` at this exact spot, and Minecraft slots your username in at runtime. So in *your* reading, the universe pauses and says your name — questioningly, like someone confirming a recognition. *Wait — {{name}}?* That one. The handle you've been carrying for years, the name your friends know you by, the name people call you across a room — it gets spoken now by something cosmic and kind.

This is also a very old religious gesture. Moses at the burning bush is called twice — *Moses, Moses*. Samuel in the temple — *Samuel, Samuel*. The divine confirms identity by speaking the name. You are not one of many here; you are being addressed.
