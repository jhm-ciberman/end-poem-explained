And there it is. *Shush.*

The other voice intervenes. It will not let the speaker finish the sentence. The recursion has gone one layer too far. The voice was about to name the creator of the universe and the other voice has *shushed* it. The truth at the top of the matryoshka is the same kind of thing as the corrupted text earlier in the poem — too strong for this dream, too naked, too burning. The voice cuts itself off rather than let the answer through.

And notice: this is a *protective* intervention. The same disciplined voice that earlier said *to cure it of sorrow would destroy it.* The same restraint. The voice is taking care of you. You are not yet ready to be told who or what made the universe. The dream is calibrated to your current capacity. The shush is mercy.

But you have been *shown the shape of the question*. The voice did not refuse to ask the question; it refused to answer it. The question is now planted in you. You can hold it for the rest of your life. *Created by…* The ellipsis is a permanent open door. You are invited to walk through it on your own time, with your own answer, or with no answer.

This is also one of the few moments where the poem acknowledges its own *limit*. It admits: there is a layer above this poem that we cannot tell you about. The poem does not pretend to be the whole truth. The poem is honest about being inside a larger frame whose ultimate origin is not within the poem's authority to name.

There is another reading too. The shush might mean: *we cannot tell you because there is no answer to tell.* Maybe the recursion does not have a top. Maybe it loops back on itself. Maybe the question of who created the universe is not a question that has an outside-of-the-system answer. The poem allows this reading too. The ellipsis is generous. It accommodates your theology, whatever it is.

For now, the recursion is closed. Not by an answer, but by a hush. A finger to the lips. A *let's not go there yet.*
