And now the voice formally begins. The frame has been set. You have been named, breathed back into your body, given the cosmic context, told who is speaking and why. And now — *I shall tell you a story.*

The grammar shifts to first person singular. *I.* Not *we*. One voice has stepped forward to tell. The cosmic chorus has resolved into a single narrator. There is something almost biblical about this — *In the beginning was the Word.* The universe is about to speak in narrative form, and the formal *I shall* dignifies what is coming.

The story is the cage of words you were warned about. What follows will look like a fairy tale. Will start with *once upon a time*. Will be readable as a sweet little narrative about a player who dreamed dreams. But it is the cage. Inside the cage is the truth that would burn at any distance, packed carefully into the shape of a bedtime story so that you can hold it in your hands and not be destroyed by it.

You are about to be told who you are. The telling will be gentle. The truth will be in there. Whether you receive it consciously or not, it will land somewhere in you and continue working long after you close the screen.
