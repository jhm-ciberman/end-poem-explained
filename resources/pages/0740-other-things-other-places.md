    text: "Sometimes the player dreamed it was other things, in other places."

And the voice opens the catalog beyond what it can name. *Other things, in other places.* Whatever else you have ever dreamed yourself to be — a child running across a yard you no longer remember, a self in a relationship that ended, a person walking through a city at four in the morning, a mind doing math that is no longer interesting — all of it. Every configuration the player has ever briefly occupied. The voice does not list them because they are too many. It just gestures at the catalog and acknowledges: *there have been many*.

This is also a quiet hint at the larger metaphysics the poem keeps almost touching. *Other things, in other places.* The voice has not committed to whether these were just imaginative episodes or whether the player has, in some deeper sense, been other things in other places. The grammar leaves the question open. The reader who needs a literal-mindfulness reading can take it; the reader who is open to reincarnation or to the multiplicity of selves can take that. The voice is not telling you which is true. It is leaving the door unlocked.
