And here the voice does the gorgeous trick of equating two scales.

You have, in your head, models of the universe at multiple scales. The very small — atoms, subatomic particles, the quantum domain — and the very large — solar systems, galaxies, cosmological structure. You learned about both in school. You have probably watched documentaries about both. You hold both in your mind.

The voice points out something that should be vertiginous. *Both models are models of flecks of energy moving through vast empty spaces.* The atom is mostly empty space; the nucleus is a tiny dense fleck and the electrons are even tinier flecks orbiting at relatively enormous distances, with vast emptiness between. The solar system is mostly empty space; the sun is a fleck and the planets are smaller flecks orbiting at relatively enormous distances, with vast emptiness between. *The structure is the same.*

You labeled the small flecks *electrons* and *protons*. You labeled the large flecks *planets* and *stars*. But the *structural truth* — flecks moving through emptiness — is the same at both scales. The voice is hinting at the old hermetic principle: *as above, so below.* The patterns repeat. The atom mirrors the solar system. The cell mirrors the city. The neuron network mirrors the galactic web. There is a shape that the universe likes to make, and it makes that shape at every scale where matter organizes itself.

Anyone who has noticed the resemblance between things at very different scales — a tree and the branching of rivers seen from a plane, the spirals of galaxies and the spirals of seashells, the way a single cell pulses and the way a city moves — has caught a glimpse of this. The voice is telling you that the *physical universe* exhibits this property — and that you, in your head, hold models that confirm it without quite noticing.
