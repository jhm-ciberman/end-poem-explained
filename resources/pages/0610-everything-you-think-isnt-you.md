And here is the line that sharpens it. The universe defines itself as *the not-self*.

Whatever you take to be *me* — your body, your thoughts, your name, your history — everything else is what the voice means by *we*. The chair. The wall. The air. The neighbor downstairs. The star whose light reaches you. The atoms you haven't eaten yet. All of that is *we*.

This is one of the cleanest definitions of *the other* that any text has ever offered. The universe is the other. *Everything you think isn't you* is the universe, because everything you think isn't you is, in fact, on the other side of the boundary you draw to define yourself.

But the voice has already told you, just a moment ago, that the boundary is *as though*. Which means the *we* and the *you* are also *as though*. The universe is everything you think isn't you, and the voice has already gently pointed out that the thinking-it-isn't-you is the dream. So the voice is naming itself in your terms — using the boundary you understand — while quietly knowing the boundary is provisional.
