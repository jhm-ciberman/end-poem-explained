    text: "The ball of molten rock circled a ball of blazing gas that was three hundred and thirty thousand times more massive than it."
---

The scale expands. Now the planet is a *ball* — small, finite — and it *circles* something far larger.

*Three hundred and thirty thousand times more massive.* The voice gives you the actual ratio. The Sun is not just *bigger*. It is bigger by a factor that exceeds intuition. If the Earth weighed one kilogram, the Sun would weigh three hundred and thirty thousand kilograms. You could fit over a million Earths inside the volume of the Sun. And your planet, your civilization, your continent, your country, your bedroom, your body — all of it is being held in the gravitational embrace of that vastly larger thing, swung around it on an invisible leash, kept at exactly the distance that permits liquid water and therefore you.

The voice gives you the number because the number does the work. *Three hundred and thirty thousand.* Specific. Verifiable. Real. The poem does not gesture vaguely at how big the sun is. It quantifies the asymmetry, and the quantification produces the awe.
