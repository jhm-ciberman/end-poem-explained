The simplest possible answer to *who are we*. Three words. *We are the universe.*

Not *we are gods*. Not *we are spirits*. Not *we are extraterrestrials*. The voice gives itself the most general possible name — and in doing so, it gives itself a name that includes everything. The universe is not one thing among many. The universe is *all things*.

So the voices that have been speaking to you throughout the poem are not aliens visiting from elsewhere. They are not gods in a separate heaven. They are *the totality*. The thing you are inside of. The thing you are made of. The thing your bedroom is part of, the thing the night sky is part of, the thing the device you are reading this on is part of. *That* is what has been talking to you.

And here is the philosophical move that follows immediately: if *we are the universe* and *you* are also part of the universe, then the conversation has been the universe talking to itself. The voices and you are not two parties separated by a screen. You are one whole, briefly arranged so as to converse.
