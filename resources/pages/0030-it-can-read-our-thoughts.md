You're "it." Not he, not she — *it*. On first read this can feel cold. But notice what it actually does: it puts you in the same grammatical register as stars, rivers, weather, atoms. You are not being demoted to object — you are being included in the cosmic inventory. The universe doesn't gender its components.

"Reached a higher level" is the poem's signature trick. It's literal — you've defeated the Ender Dragon and triggered the credits — but it's also ancient mystical language for spiritual ascension, gnosis, awakening. The poem is going to do this constantly: take a phrase that means one thing inside a video game and let it bloom into something the game was secretly a metaphor for.

"It can read our thoughts" — because you, right now, are literally reading the dialogue on a screen. The fourth wall is porous. Every layer of this poem knows about every other layer. That recursion is its engine.
